Customer Churn & Retention Analysis (Excel and Power BI Project)

 Project Overview

. This project analyzes customer subscription data to understand churn and retention. The analysis was done using excel for data cleaning and the Power Bi for visualization and reporting.

1. Project Objectives

. Understand churn and retention trends

. Explore customer behavior based on contracts and services

. Study customer lifetime patterns

. Identify reasons why customers leave

. Provide recommendations to reduce churn

2. Key Insights

2.1. Overall Churn

. Most customers did not churn (about 73%)

. Around 27% of customers churned

This shows that even though most customers stay, churn is still a problem for the business

2.2 Customer Tenure

. Customers who did not churn have higher tenure

. Customers who churned have lower tenure

This shows that new customers are more likely to leave early

2.3 Contract Type

. Month-to-month contracts have the highest churn

. One-year contracts have lower churn

. Two-year contracts have the lowest churn

This means customers with long-term contracts are more likely to stay

2.4 Customer Services

. Customers without tech support are more likely to churn

. Customers with tech support are fewer but more stable

This shows that support services can help reduce churn

2.5 Customer Lifetime Patterns

Customers who stay longer are more stable

Customers who churn usually leave in the early stage

This means the first few months are very important for retention

2.6 Internet Service

. Most customers use fiber optic service

. DSL is second

. Customers with no internet service are fewer

This shows fiber is the most preferred service, but it may still have churn issues

3. Recommendations

3.1 Improve Early Customer Experience:

. Focus on new customers in their first months

. Provide better onboarding and support

3.2 Promote Long-Term Contracts:

. Encourage customers to move from monthly to yearly plans

. Offer small discounts or benefits

3.3 Reduce Month-to-Month Churn:

. Give incentives for switching plans

. Offer loyalty rewards

3.4 Increase Use of Tech Support:

. Make support services more visible and easy to use

3.5 Monitor High-Risk Customers:

. Track new customers and customers with short tenure

Conclusion

. Churn mostly happens with new customers and customers on month-to-month contracts

. Long-term customers are more stable

. Support services and contracts play an important role in retention

The business should focus on early customer experience and long-term plans to reduce churn and improve customer lifetime value
